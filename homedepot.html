    Our History

    Bernie Marcus and Arthur Blank dreamed up The Home Depot from a coffee shop in Los Angeles in 1978.


    Avid DIYers, they envisioned a superstore that would offer a huge variety of merchandise at great prices and with a highly trained staff. With help from investment banker Ken Langone and merchandising guru Pat Farrah, Marcus and Blank opened the first two Home Depot stores in Atlanta the following year, and the rest is history.

    From those two stores in Atlanta, Home Depot has grown to more than 2,298 stores in three countries, but in every store, our goal is the same: to earn our customers’ respect by offering the highest level of service, the broadest selection of products and the most competitive prices.

    Our Values


    Our values are represented by a wheel to signify how each work together, like spokes, to keep us moving forward.

    They consist of Excellent Customer Service, Creating Shareholder Value, Entrepreneurial Spirit, Taking Care of Our People, Respect for All People, Doing the Right Thing, Building Strong Relationships, and Giving Back.

    The Values Wheel is ingrained in every part of The Home Depot’s culture. It decorates breakrooms and adorns aprons. It has been translated into Spanish, French and Chinese, amongst other languages. It inspires associates and shows customers what we stand for. The Values Wheel represents the orange-blooded culture that was at our core from the very beginning.

    The Orange Method

    The OrangeMethod is a training program that offers technology training for current and new associates.


    The Home Depot implemented OrangeMethod into their onboarding process to ensure all necessary tools and resources are readily available to associates. The tech boot camp first started as a 12-week program centered around software engineering. The latest iteration is a 16-week course that associates can use to become UX managers, product managers, or software engineers.
